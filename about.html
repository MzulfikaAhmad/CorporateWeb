About PERKIM Selangor

Understand the organisation's history, core direction and role in supporting outreach, education and welfare in Selangor.

Lets start to know...

A state division within a national Islamic welfare organisation

The Muslim Welfare Organisation of Malaysia, commonly known as PERKIM, was founded by Tunku Abdul Rahman Putra Al-Haj and registered on 19 August 1960. The Selangor division states that it was established in 1964.

PERKIM operates through state divisions and local branches. PERKIM Bahagian Negeri Selangor coordinates programmes across Selangor and maintains its office at Masjid Sultan Salahuddin Abdul Aziz Shah in Shah Alam.

Sources: PERKIM national background ↗ and PERKIM Selangor Infaq portal .

Website awareness objectives

  • Present accurate organisational information in accessible English.
  • Promote safe participation in community and volunteer activities.
  • Direct donations only to the verified official Infaq channel.
  • Help visitors distinguish the PERKIM office from the mosque management office.

Historical overview

Key verified milestones

19 August 1960

PERKIM registered nationally

PERKIM was founded by Malaysia's first Prime Minister, Tunku Abdul Rahman Putra Al-Haj.

1964

Selangor division established

The PERKIM Selangor official donation portal identifies 1964 as its year of establishment.

11 March 1988

Shah Alam state mosque officially opened

Masjid Sultan Salahuddin Abdul Aziz Shah was officially inaugurated and later became the address of the PERKIM Selangor office.

2025

Strong role in new-Muslim registration

MAIS reported that PERKIM Selangor recorded the highest number of conversions among its listed registration management centres for 2025.
